Critical Warning Signs

These signs indicate high risk, seek help immediately.

  • ⚠ Escalating violence or threats
  • ⚠ Extreme jealousy and controlling behavior
  • ⚠ Threats to kill or harm you
  • ⚠ Strangulation attempts
  • ⚠ Physical threats with weapons
  • ⚠ Separation or attempts to leave abusive partner
  • ⚠ Stalking
  • ⚠ Substance abuse combined with violence
  • ⚠ Rape/forced sexual acts
  • ⚠ Threats to harm children or pets

If You're In Danger

  • ✓ Call emergency services if in immediate danger
  • ✓ Go to a safe location away from the abuser
  • ✓ Inform trusted friends or family about your situation
  • ✓ Document all incidents with dates and details
  • ✓ Seek a protection/restraining order against the abuser

Safety Planning

  • ✓ Identify safe places to go in an emergency
  • ✓ Keep important documents and emergency cash accessible
  • ✓ Keep emergency contacts readily accessible
  • ✓ Have a packed bag ready with essentials
  • ✓ Trust your instincts and prioritize your safety

Fighting FGM in Kenya

Zero tolerance for Female Genital Mutilation


What is FGM?

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) involves the partial or total removal of external female genitalia for non-medical reasons. It is illegal in Kenya under the Prohibition of Female Genital Mutilation Act 2011, with penalties of up to 3 years imprisonment or a fine of KES 200,000.


What Is the Prevalence of FGM in Kenya?

According to the 2014 Kenya Demographic and Health Survey (KDHS), the prevalence of FGM in Kisii County was at 84% against the national prevalence of 21%.


Why is FGM harmful?

FGM has no health benefits and causes severe immediate and long-term physical and psychological harm including severe pain, infections, complications in childbirth, psychological trauma, and can be fatal.


How Can I Report FGM?

Report to the police (999), Anti-FGM Board (+254 20 2211 441), Child Helpline (116), or any of the GBV helplines. All reports are treated seriously and confidentially.


Support for FGM Survivors

Medical reconstruction, psychological counseling, legal support, and community reintegration programs are available through specialised centers and organisations.
